Radical

Kanji Made Radically Easy

Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Some radicals show the meaning, while others indicate the on’yomi reading, used when two kanji are together. For example, 泊まる means "to stay" and is read とまる. With another kanji, it's read ハク, because it contains the phonetic radical 白. Similarly, 伯, 迫, and 拍 are read ハク but have different meanings based on their radicals.
