Radical.

Kanji Made Radically Easy.

Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Some radicals show the meaning, while others indicate the on’yomi reading, used when two kanji are together. For example, 泊まる means "to stay" and is read とまる. When 泊 is next to another kanji, it's read ハク, because it contains the phonetic radical 白.
