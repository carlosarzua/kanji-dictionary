Radical. v.1.0*

Kanji Made Radically Easier.

Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Some radicals show the meaning, while others indicate the on’yomi reading, used when two kanji are together. For example, 泊まる means "to stay" and is read とまる. When 泊 is next to another kanji, it's read ハク, because it contains the phonetic radical 白. Similarly, 伯, 迫, and 拍 are read ハク but have different meanings based on their meaning radicals. None of them have to do with 'white'.

*Version 1.0 [released Oct 18, 2024] includes a set of 279 radicals and 1148 kanji. The dictionary does not yet show kun'yomi readings, an English translation, or meaning radicals.These features will soon be released and an app development is in progress as well, with features such as tracking kanji & radical acquisition progress, tests, and kanji classification by JLPT level.
