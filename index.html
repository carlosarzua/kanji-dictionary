Radical

Kanji Made Radically Easy.

The character зҙ…

contains the phonetic radical е·Ҙ

which is read гӮігӮҰ.

Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Some radicals show the meaning, while others indicate the onвҖҷyomi reading, used when two kanji are together. For example, жіҠгҒҫгӮӢ means "to stay" and is read гҒЁгҒҫгӮӢ. When жіҠ is next to another kanji, it's read гғҸгӮҜ, because it contains the phonetic radical зҷҪ. Similarly, дјҜ, иҝ«, and жӢҚ are read гғҸгӮҜ but have different meanings based on their radicals.
