Radical
Kanji Made Radically Easy
Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Some radicals indicate meaning, like 木 ('tree') in 森 ('forest'). However, other radicals, known as phonetic radicals, give the on’yomi reading when two kanji are together. For example, 白 ('white') in 泊 is not about color but provides the reading ハク, used in other kanji like 伯, 拍, and 迫. 工
contains the phonetic radical 工
, which is read コウ
